## 0. GOVERNING NARRATIVE (Slide 2 — "the story in four moves")

Per desk guidance, the deck opens on a single narrative arc before the three-pillar summary:

1. **The setup** — markets were *primed to rally*: post-election stability (BNP government since Feb-26), inflation rolling over from its 2024–25 peak, and the market positioned for the first rate cut.
2. **The shock** — the **Middle East (Iran–Israel) war** spiked energy/LNG costs, knocked the inflation and policy-rate trajectory off track, and **pushed the easing cycle — and the equity re-rating — back by at least 6 months. Delayed, not derailed.**
3. **The valuation** — the delay leaves the market *cheaper than at the setup*: **8.5x trailing P/E**, ~30% below the 10-yr average, ~48% below Vietnam, **+42% re-rating upside** to the long-run mean (ex-EPS growth).
4. **The changes** — reforms keep compounding through the wait: BSEC rulebook reset + new chairman, CCBL/T+1 accessibility track, and sukuk carry (~10.4%) that pays you to be patient.

**Net:** the catalyst is delayed, not cancelled, and the entry is cheaper than when the thesis began.

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (Minto governing thought)

> **Bangladesh equities trade at 8.5x trailing P/E — a ~30% discount to the 10-year average and ~48% discount to Vietnam — even as a multi-year regulatory reset, settlement-cycle upgrade and a stabilising (if war-delayed) macro create the conditions for a re-rating. We see asymmetric upside for patient capital willing to underwrite continued reform execution under the new government and incoming BSEC leadership.**

Three supporting pillars:
1. **Cheap on every absolute and relative metric** (P/E, drawdown, mkt-cap/GDP).
2. **Cyclical headwinds are visible, dated, and increasingly priced in** (10% repo, easing cycle pushed back ~6m by the energy shock, 30%+ NPL recognition, war-driven LNG shortfall).
3. **Structural tailwinds compound** (BSEC reset and pending leadership change, floor-price removal, CCBL/T+1 plumbing, REIT/sukuk depth, MSCI monitoring, FY27 budget capital-market push).

## 3. HEADWINDS (for Slide 5)

### 3a. Macro
- **GDP FY26: 3.9%** (World Bank Bangladesh Development Update, 8 Apr 2026) — repeatedly downgraded from initial 6.5%. ADB 4.0%, IMF 4.9%, UN 4.6%.
- **CPI: 8.5% (FY26 avg, World Bank)** with food and non-food both elevated; April 2026 print 9.04%. BB target <7%.
- **Policy repo: 10.00%** (held in Jan-Jun FY26 MPS); SDF cut 50bp to 7.5% in Jan 2026.
- **Lending rates:** ~13.5–15%.
- **Private sector credit growth: 6.03% YoY (Feb 2026)** — 5-year low vs BB target 8.5%.
- **FX reserves:** Gross USD 25.70 bn (24 May 2026 print); BPM6 ~USD 20.47 bn. Earlier IMF-method run had peaked ~USD 35 bn in Feb 2026 before slipping as IMF disbursements paused pending political transition.
- BDT ~120/USD; FY24 saw 8.17% depreciation.
- **Remittance bright spot:** Jul-25 to mid-May-26: USD 25.45 bn (+28% YoY); record monthly USD 3.29 bn in Mar 2026.

### 3b. IMF program
- USD 5.5 bn ECF/EFF/RSF (Jan 2023); augmented in 2025 to SDR 3,035 mn + SDR 1 bn.
- Combined 3rd & 4th reviews completed 23 Jun 2025 (USD 1.3 bn unlocked).
- **2025 Article IV concluded 26 Jan 2026**; 5th-review staff discussions completed Nov 2025.
- **Program now extended six months to 27 Jan 2027** (from original Jul 2026 end), at GoB request.
- IMF flagged: weak revenue mobilisation, banking-sector vulnerabilities, incomplete FX framework, sticky inflation.
- **Risk:** Next tranche (~USD 400 mn) decision sits with new BNP government; successor program articulation still pending.

### 3c. Geopolitics — four-front squeeze
- **US tariffs (Trump 2.0):** 19% reciprocal tariff under Feb 2026 framework (was 37% in Apr 2025, 20% Aug 2025). US absorbs ~20% of RMG; US apparel imports from BD = USD 8.18 bn (2025). Buyers seeking 2% price cuts.
- **Iran-Israel war (escalated 2026):** Bangladesh imports 95% of energy. **40 of 115 contracted 2026 LNG cargoes expected lost** due to Strait of Hormuz disruption. QatarEnergy invoked force majeure. Govt imposed fuel rationing and shut universities (Mar 2026).
- **Red Sea:** Chattogram-Europe/US freight up 20–50%, +10 days transit. ~70% of RMG goes to EU.
- **India tensions:** Visa services mutually suspended 22 Dec 2025; CEPA talks shelved; May 2025 Indian curbs affect ~42% of BD imports from India.
- **Myanmar/Rakhine:** ~150,000 new Rohingya since early 2024; Arakan Army incursions into CHT (Feb 2026).
- **Russia-Ukraine:** ~50% of FY24 wheat from Russia; urea prices remain elevated.

### 3d. Banking
- **System NPL: 30.6%** (Dec 2025, WB Apr-26 BDU) — likely highest globally. SoCBs alone hold ~45% of stock.
- **Islami Bank:** Bad loans Tk 94,322 cr in 2025 (+44% YoY); NPL = 51%; CRAR 6.42% vs 12.5% req.
- S Alam Group exposure is the proximate driver; mergers under way.

### 3e. Fuel pricing
- Automatic monthly formula introduced Mar 2024 (IMF condition).
- April 2026 retail (Tk/L): Diesel 115, Kerosene 130, Petrol 135, Octane 140.
- March 2026: prices held unchanged despite global volatility — CPD flags this as discretionary suspension.

## 4. TAILWINDS (for Slides 6 & 7)

### 4a. BSEC reset & pending leadership transition
- **Aug 2024:** Khondoker Rashed Maqsood appointed BSEC chairman following the political transition; rulebook overhaul commenced.
- **2024–25 enforcement record:** ~Tk 1,500 cr cumulative manipulation fines — the largest sweep in BSEC's history.
  - **Beximco / Salman F. Rahman:** Tk 428 cr fine; banned from capital market.
  - **Shakib Al Hasan / Abul Khayer syndicate:** Tk 194 cr fine.
- Recovery only ~0.35% (Tk 5.23 cr); most penalties stayed in HC. **Enforcement record is institutional groundwork rather than realised cash.**
- **Leadership transition under way (May 2026):** Tenure of current chairman drawing to a close amid persistent market weakness; press reporting flags Masud Khan (Chairman, Unilever Consumer Care; ex-Lafarge-Holcim CFO) as the leading candidate. Govt is removing the age-limit bar in the BSEC Act to accommodate a senior, market-credible appointment. **A clean handover and a non-political technocrat appointment would be a positive signal for foreign portfolio sentiment** ahead of the FY27 budget and the MSCI Nov-26 review.

### 4b. Floor-price removal (21 Jan 2024)
- Floor prices imposed Jul 2022 had trapped >60% of scrips with zero trading.
- 21 Jan 2024: floors lifted on all but 35 stocks; subsequent tranches cleared most. Lower circuit breaker reset to 10%.
- **MSCI confirmed (24 Jun 2025) floor prices removed for all but 2 securities** — major accessibility tick.

### 4c. Margin Loan Rules 2025 (gazetted 6 Nov 2025)
- Max margin ratio 1:1.
- Single-client cap 10% of lender capital; 30% sector cap; 25% single-stock cap.
- Ban on margin-loan-financed directorships.

### 4d. CCBL (Central Counterparty Bangladesh Ltd)
- Incorporated Jan 2019 under Clearing & Settlement Rules 2017.
- Owned by DSE, CSE, CDBL.
- Still not operational late 2024 / early 2025 — director-appointment delays cost 955 days.
- BSEC publicly targeted CCBL + derivatives launch by 2025 — slipping but on roadmap.

### 4e. T+1 settlement
- Currently T+2 for A/B-category stocks.
- BSEC first moved on T+1 in 2022; DSE re-raised with BB Governor Mansur in 2025.
- No firm 2026 go-live yet, but it's the standard MSCI/FTSE accessibility tick. India has been T+1 since 2023.

### 4f. Bond / Sukuk market
- T-bonds live on DSE/CSE since 10 Oct 2022. 2024 DSE T-bond turnover Tk 1.31 bn (+55% YoY).
- Cumulative sovereign sukuk issuance ~Tk 22,000 cr by mid-2025.
- 5th sovereign sukuk: BDT 3,000 cr (rural bridges).
- Jun 2024 CDWSP Social Impact Sukuk: 5-yr, 10.40% rental, 2.5x oversubscribed.

### 4g. REITs, ETFs, Mutual Funds
- **REIT Fund Rules 2024:** Min Tk 200 cr (Tk 300 cr in city corps); sponsor ≥20%; 90% dividend mandate; 3-yr lock-in.
- **Mutual Fund Rules 2025 (draft):** Closed-end tenure cap 10 yrs; mandatory EGM if price falls >25% below issue/NAV.
- **ETFs:** Shariah-ETF guidelines approved late 2024. No scaled live product yet.

### 4h. Foreign investor access
- NITA account remains gateway; **free repatriation of listed-share proceeds (no prior BB approval).**
- AY-26 (from 1 Jul 2025): flat **15% CGT** on listed-share gains for non-residents.
- DSE proposal to FY27 budget: **5-year CGT exemption for NRBs**; cut CGT for individuals to 5% (from 15% over Tk 50 lakh).

### 4i. MSCI / FTSE
- **MSCI 2025 Review (24 Jun 2025):** Bangladesh retained **Frontier**; "continuing to monitor"; floor-price progress noted positively.
- Next read: MSCI Nov 2026 semi-annual review.

### 4j. FY27 Budget (Slide 8 catalyst)
- **First budget of the BNP-led 13th JS government** (Finance Minister Amir Khosru Mahmud Chowdhury).
- Budget session convenes **7 Jun 2026**; budget speech expected **11 Jun 2026**; passage by **30 Jun 2026**.
- **Headline size:** ~Tk 9.30 lakh crore (Tk 3 lakh cr development + Tk 6.30 lakh cr operating).
- **Targets:** GDP growth 6.5%; inflation 7.5%; investment 31.4% of GDP; revenue 10.17% of GDP.
- **12 priority sectors** including education, healthcare, jobs, food & energy security, SMEs, agri.
- **Capital-market asks queued for the budget:**
  - DSE: 5-yr CGT exemption for NRBs; cut individual CGT to 5%; lower AIT on share trading.
  - DBA: end double taxation; treat dividend TDS as final.
  - ABB: cut bank corporate tax to ≤30%; tax-free T-bill/T-bond capital gains.
  - Industry: widen listed-vs-unlisted corporate-tax gap; mandatory listings for large firms.
- **What to watch:** any reciprocal IMF-program signal in the speech (continuity vs renegotiation), and whether even one major equity-market tax ask is honoured.

## 5. INVESTMENT THESIS (for Slide 8)

**Positioning:** OW on selective Bangladesh equities for 12–18m horizon.

**Preferred entry routes:**
- Quality large-caps with USD-linked or remittance-leveraged earnings (Pharma exporters, Banks with strong CRAR).
- Sukuk for fixed-income carry (10.40% rental).
- Avoid: high-NPL Islamic banks, telecoms, NBFI.

**Key catalysts (next 6–12m):**
1. New BSEC chairman appointment & first 100-day signal.
2. FY27 budget (11 Jun 2026) — capital-market tax relief & IMF program continuity signal.
3. CCBL operationalisation + T+1 announcement.
4. MSCI Nov 2026 semi-annual review.
5. Inflation falling below 7% → policy rate cuts.

**Key risks:**
1. IMF 5th-tranche delay / successor program lapse.
2. Further LNG / power outages from Middle East war.
3. NPL recognition wave hitting bank earnings.
4. US tariff renegotiation in 2026.
5. Disorderly BSEC leadership transition.
